The Facial Sculptor

I don't treat the face like a surface.
I treat it like a signal.

Jaw tension, tightness behind the eyes, headaches, clenching, that "stuck" feeling in the neck and face — these aren't random. They're patterns the nervous system repeats until someone helps the body interrupt them.

My work is designed to unwind what the face is holding and restore what it's been missing: circulation, softness, and mobility. When the system downshifts, structure returns on its own. I call this the sculpt.

This is where people come when a regular facial, a bodywork session, or "just relax" hasn't been enough.
